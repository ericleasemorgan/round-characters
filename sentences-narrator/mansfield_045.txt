He is just come, my dear Fanny, and is taken upstairs; and I am so shocked to see him, that I do not know what to do.
Within a few days from the receipt of Edmund ’s letter, Fanny had one from her aunt, beginning thus — My Dear Fanny, — I take up my pen to communicate some very alarming intelligence, which I make no doubt will give you much concern.
Three or four Prices might have been swept away, any or all except Fanny and William, and Lady Bertram would have thought little about it; or perhaps might have caught from Mrs. Norris ’s lips the cant of its being a very happy thing and a great blessing to their poor dear sister Price to have them so well provided for.
