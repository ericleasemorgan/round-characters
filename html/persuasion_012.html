he (Cobb) cried. "Oh God! that I had not given way to her (Henrietta) at the fatal moment! Had I done as I ought! But so eager and so resolute! Dear, sweet Louisa!"

Anne wondered whether it ever occurred to him (Cobb) now, to question the justness of his (Cobb) own previous opinion as to the universal felicity and advantage of firmness of character; and whether it might not strike him (Cobb) that, like all other qualities of the mind, it should have its proportions and limits. She (Anne) thought it could scarcely escape him (Cobb) to feel that a persuadable temper might sometimes be as much in favour of happiness as a very resolute character.

They got on fast. Anne was astonished to recognise the same hills and the same objects so soon. Their actual speed, heightened by some dread of the conclusion, made the road appear but half as long as on the day before. It was growing quite dusk, however, before they were in the neighbourhood of Uppercross, and there had been total silence among them for some time, Henrietta leaning back in the corner, with a shawl over her (Henrietta) face, giving the hope of her (Henrietta) having cried herself to sleep; when, as they were going up their last hill, Anne found herself all at once addressed by Captain Wentworth. In a low, cautious voice, he (Wentworth) said:--

"I have been considering what we had best do. She (Anne) must not appear at first. She (Anne) could not stand it. I have been thinking whether you had not better remain in the carriage with her (Anne), while I go in and break it to Mr and Mrs Musgrove. Do you think this is a good plan?"

She (Musgrove) did: he (Mr) was satisfied, and said no more. But the remembrance of the appeal remained a pleasure to her (Musgrove), as a proof of friendship, and of deference for her (Musgrove) judgement, a great pleasure; and when it became a sort of parting proof, its value did not lessen.

When the distressing communication at Uppercross was over, and he (Mr) had seen the father and mother quite as composed as could be hoped, and the daughter all the better for being with them, he (Mr) announced his (Mr) intention of returning in the same carriage to Lyme; and when the horses were baited, he (Mr) was off.
